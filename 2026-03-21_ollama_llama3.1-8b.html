Ongoing Submitted 2026-03-18 ⏱ No comments yet [PATCH v3 3/4] locking: Add contended_release tracepoint to sleepable locks Neutral llm-chunked-no-replies Awaiting Review

This patch adds a new tracepoint called contended_release to the Linux kernel's locking subsystem. The contended_release tracepoint fires on the holder side when a contended lock is released, allowing for correlation of lock hold time under contention with waiter events by lock address. The patch modifies various slowpath unlock paths in sleepable locks (mutex, rtmutex, semaphore, rwsem, percpu-rwsem, and RT-specific rwbase locks) to fire the tracepoint before or after the lock is released, depending on the specific lock type. This enables more detailed tracing of locking behavior.

Ongoing Submitted 2026-03-18 ⏱ No comments yet [RFC PATCH v3 4/4] locking: Add contended_release tracepoint to spinning locks Positive llm-chunked-no-replies RFC

This patch adds a contended_release tracepoint to spinning locks, specifically queued spinlocks and rwlocks. The tracepoint is designed to minimize the impact on the hot path when enabled, using an out-of-line function that combines contention check, trace call, and unlock operations. When disabled, only a single NOP instruction is added to the hot path. The patch also includes changes to handle inlined unlock cases, which could not be achieved through Kconfig options alone.

Ongoing Submitted 2026-03-08 🔥 TODAY [PATCH] md/raid0: use kvzalloc/kvfree for strip_zone and devlist allocations Positive LLM Under Review

This patch addresses a WARNING in the RAID0 driver caused by large allocation sizes exceeding MAX_PAGE_ORDER, leading to a WARN_ON_ONCE_GFP condition. The patch converts strip_zone and devlist allocations from kzalloc/kzalloc_objs to kvzalloc/kvfree, which first attempts contiguous allocation with __GFP_NOWARN and falls back to vmalloc for large sizes.
