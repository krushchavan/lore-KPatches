Patch summary
This patch addresses a regression in Btrfs where PAGECACHE_TAG_DIRTY is never cleared, leading to potential issues such as csum corruption when mmap writes are allowed on folios under writeback. The problem arises from the removal of folio_clear_dirty_for_io() and folio_mkclean(), which previously ensured that folios were write-protected during writeback. To fix this, the patch likely takes a lock in writeback to prevent races between truncate and writeback operations, ensuring that csums are correctly computed.

Qu Wenruo 2026-07-07 Inline Review Reviewed-by Needs Work LLM
Reviewer Qu Wenruo pointed out that the patch's approach to clearing PAGECACHE_TAG_DIRTY is flawed, as it creates a timing issue between folio dirty and writeback flags changes. Specifically, if the folio dirty flag is cleared early, it can lead to losing the TOWRITE tag for the last block; if it's not cleared until the last dirty block clearing, folio_start_writeback() may have already been called, preventing the TOWRITE tag from being cleared. Reviewer Qu Wenruo questioned the necessity of calling btrfs_mark_ordered_io_finished(), pointing out that the folio is already locked and the timing difference should not cause issues related to this function. Reviewer Qu Wenruo noted that the patch introduces a deadlock caused by clearing dirty pages without calling btrfs_mark_ordered_io_finished(), which leads to leaked unfinished ordered extents and potential hangs on test cases btrfs/062 and btrfs/070. He suggested using folio_mkclean() instead of folio_clear_dirty_for_io() as a possible solution. Reviewer Qu Wenruo identified two serious bugs in the original patch: a bug that causes clearing TOWRITE on non-sync writebacks and losing it for other pages in the folio, and a deadlock caused by truncating pages past the new file size without calling btrfs_mark_ordered_io_finished(). He suggested de-coupling PAGECACHE_TAG_DIRTY/TOWRITE from folio_*_writeback() calls to fix these issues.

But there is a deeper problem, the timing of folio dirty and writeback
