Patch summary
This patch modifies the hopeless kswapd mechanism in Linux's memory management system. The goal is to prevent kswapd from becoming overly aggressive when global direct reclaim makes progress, which can lead to an imbalance between watermark levels and actual memory availability. The patch addresses a specific bug where a single direct reclaimer getting lucky triggers a burst of kswapd activity, causing problems even if the unreclaimable set exceeds available memory.

It does, and I see it in the runs: the patched kswapd latches and
unlatches in 16-pass bursts, one per direct reclaim success, where the
baseline latches once and stays down. Each burst is bounded - the
revived kswapd hits MAX_RECLAIM_RETRIES zero-reclaim runs and parks
again. Keeping it up takes a new success every time - a task in the
slowpath, and something left to reclaim.

---

No progress event (global or memcg) says kswapd can balance the node.
The difference is what sets them off. memory.high re-triggers on the
cgroup's own usage (whatever the node is doing) - so a cgroup parked
at its limit keeps kswapd cycling even when the node needs nothing.
OTOH global direct reclaim only runs when the page allocator itself is
below the watermarks. The workload behind those numbers has no memcg
reclaim in it at all - the gate went in for the memory.high, and it is
parking kswapd here.

---

Under sustained pressure this node does not balance either - thats the
workload this came from. kswapd keeps running there for as long as the
node stays starved, and that is what the patch is for. The reclaim has
to happen either way, the only question is whether every allocating task
does it synchronously or kswapd does it in the background. That is the
413441 -> 6532 in the changelog, with pgsteal moving from direct to
kswapd. When the demand stops, the resets stop with it and kswapd parks.

---

The changelog cites dc9fe9b7056a and the any-progress behaviour before
it. c73322d098e4, which introduced the counter, describes kswapd
sleeping "until a direct reclaimer manages to reclaim some pages, thus
proving the node reclaimable again".

That leaves the tradeoff: either the balanced-only reset stays and the
observed allocation stalls remain, or global direct reclaim may revive
kswapd and that work moves back to the background.
