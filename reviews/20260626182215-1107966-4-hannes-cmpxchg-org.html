[PATCH 3/4] mm: page_alloc: move capture_control to the page allocator

Patch summary
This patch moves the per-task capture_control to the page allocator, allowing it to carry allocation-side information that compaction's compact_control does not. The capture_control is passed through try_to_compact_pages() and compact_zone_order() instead of a bare struct page **. This change is necessary because defrag_mode promotes sub-block allocations to pageblock-order compaction, which targets the larger order, while capture should remain at the original allocation order. No functional changes are made by this patch.
Johannes Weiner (author) 2026-07-01 Neutral LLM
Author acknowledged that the swapoff path needs to drop the per-vswap spinlock before calling try_to_unmap(), agreed to restructure in v2 Author addressed a concern about the capture_control struct being passed through try_to_compact_pages() and compact_zone_order(), explaining that they added 'struct zone' and 'int migratetype' fields to it, and provided an updated diff showing these changes. Author acknowledged that initializing capture_control partially would zero out its other fields, but didn't see it as a correctness issue; no fix planned.
