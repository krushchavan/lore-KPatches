[PATCH v7 03/15] mm/mglru: relocate the LRU scan batch limit to callers

View on lore.kernel.org →
Submitted: 2026-05-29
Active on: 2026-05-29
Patch summary
The patch relocates the LRU scan batch limit to callers, but raises concerns about isolating a large chunk of folios off the list and potential livelock issues.
Shakeel Butt 2026-05-29 Acked-by Neutral LLM
Acknowledged the concerns, but questioned whether a warning is necessary and suggested that the limitation could be removed. Acked the patch.

> Do we really need a warning here? Also why are we limiting it to MAX_LRU_BATCH?
> For memcg/proactive reclaim, we can get larger number. What will break if we
> remove this limitation?

Hi,
Isolating a large chunk of folios off the list is usually a bad idea,
livelock is one concern, besides, concurrent reclaimer won't see them
anymore, and LRU operations on them will be skipped (eg. roration).
Under heavy pressure, this could lead to premature OOM because some
reclaimers will see the LRU as empty. or accuracy loss.

There is a LRU isolate throttle for CLRU, which is missing for MGLRU,
so just being careful here, can be removed anytime, but I won't think
isolating more than MAX_LRU_BATCH for a single time will ever be a
good idea.
