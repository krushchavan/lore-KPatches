[PATCH] input/evdev: move kill_fasync() outside buffer_lock to fix SOFTIRQ deadlock

View on lore.kernel.org →
Submitted: 2026-05-13  ·  Last comment: 2026-05-14
Active on: 2026-05-14 • 2026-05-13
Patch summary
This patch fixes a potential deadlock in the Linux kernel's input/evdev driver by moving the call to kill_fasync() outside of the buffer_lock spinlock. The buffer_lock is SOFTIRQ-safe, but kill_fasync() acquires a SOFTIRQ-unsafe lock, creating a potential ordering violation that can lead to a deadlock. By moving the kill_fasync() call after the buffer_lock is released, the patch resolves this issue and prevents the deadlock from occurring.
Dmitry Torokhov 2026-05-13 Inline Review Reviewed-by Needs Work LLM
reviewer noted that the patch does not actually fix the SOFTIRQ deadlock because it still acquires fa_lock while holding buffer_lock in evdev_pass_values(), and requested the lock be dropped before calling wake_up_interruptible_poll() reviewer questioned whether the patch actually fixes the issue, pointing out that the code runs while holding input->event_lock with IRQs disabled
